Short Biography

Founder and advisor building digital products at the intersection of social justice, technology, education, & community

Monica S. Flores works with U.S. and international groups on digital solutions that build community, foster a feeling of connectedness, and help make the world a better place.

Author of the A Successful Woman’s Handbook series, she is passionate about leadership, technology, and how to #makeapositivedifference. She speaks and presents to national audiences.

Full Biography

Monica S. Flores brings over fifteen years of experience as a web developer, engineering manager, digital products director, and advisor to national and global teams. Most recent engagements include her roles as director and lead software engineer for large-scale redesign and refactoring for Green America, Ashoka Changemakers, and the Global Entrepreneurship Network for the U.S. Department of State.

She supports leadership and engineering teams on product lifecycle development for projects that make a positive difference. She helps identify functional specifications, plans complex database architecture, defines content types, understands user permissions, determines reporting needs, and manages system-wide processes.

Author of the A Successful Woman’s Handbook series, Flores is passionate about leadership, innovation, education, and technology. She writes, speaks, and presents on social justice, environmental sustainability, and entrepreneurship and currently serves on the faculty for the Nonprofit Technology Network's Professional Certification, teaching "Building a Culture of Diversity and Inclusion" and "Advanced Data Management."

She advises emerging projects that focus on building community, fostering connectedness, and advancing technology to create a better world. She is a worker board member representing Green America staff. She co-founded 10K Group web consulting, where since 2004 she has delivered 240+ projects for business, government, and education. She founded and sold A Successful Woman (2006-2012), co-founded Gramercy & Co (2010-2017), and currently runs Female Founders Lead the Way and PosDiff.

She received a B.S. from the Department of Agricultural and Biological Engineering at Cornell University, and is currently based in the Washington DC area, where she lives with her family.
